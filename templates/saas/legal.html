These terms cover the Focal hosted service ("Focal", "we"). By accepting an invitation or using a hosted studio you agree to them. If you self-host Focal, these hosted terms don't apply — you run your own instance under its open-source licence.

Beta access

Focal hosted access is invitation-only while the product is in private beta. Any pricing, trial length, and availability terms are stated in the invitation or the current order flow. The product is not generally available.

Your studio, your data

Each studio runs in its own isolated database and file store — not shared rows in a common table. You own the content you upload and create (photos, videos, client records, invoices). You can export a complete archive of your studio, or delete it, at any time from your billing page. Deleting cancels billing and takes the studio offline; we keep a short recoverable copy in case of mistakes, then remove it.

Acceptable use

Don't use Focal to store unlawful content, infringe others' rights, or attempt to access another studio's data. Each studio is isolated by design; probing that isolation is a breach of these terms. We may suspend a studio that puts the service or other customers at risk, and will tell you why.

Payments

Billing is handled by Stripe; we don't store your card number. Client payments you collect through your own connected payment account are between you and your clients — Focal is not a party to them and never charges your clients on its own account.

Availability & liability

We work to keep Focal available and backed up, but it's provided "as is" without warranty. To the extent the law allows, our liability is limited to the fees you paid in the prior three months. You're responsible for keeping your own export archives for anything you can't afford to lose.

Changes

We may update these terms; material changes will be announced in-app before they take effect. Continued use after that means you accept the update.

{% elif doc == "privacy" %}

This policy explains what the Focal hosted service collects and how it's used. Self-hosted installs are governed by whoever runs them, not this policy.

What we hold

  • Account data — your studio name, owner email, and a hashed password.
  • Billing data — a Stripe customer/subscription reference. Card details live with Stripe, not with us.
  • Studio content — everything you put into your studio (clients, projects, galleries, invoices, uploaded media), stored in your studio's own isolated database and file store.

How it's used

To run your studio, take payment for the subscription, and provide support. We do not sell your data, and we do not use your client media or studio content to train AI models. AI features act only on your own content, at your request, and every AI output is a draft you approve before anything is sent or published.

Isolation & access

Studios are physically isolated (separate database + file store per customer). Operator access to your content is limited to what's needed to run the service and respond to your support requests.

Sharing

We share data only with the processors that run the service — currently Stripe (billing) and email delivery for transactional mail (e.g. password resets) — and only as needed. We disclose data if legally required, and will tell you unless prohibited.

Common things you can do yourself

  • Forgot your password? Use the "Forgot password?" link on your studio's sign-in page — a reset link goes to your owner email.
  • Update billing or cancel? Your Billing page has the Stripe portal and the cancel/delete controls.
  • Leaving? Download a complete archive of your studio from the Billing page first — it includes your database and all media.

Trouble signing in

If a payment lapses, your studio stays reachable through a short grace period so you can update billing before anything is locked. If you're fully locked out, the sign-in and billing pages still work so you can recover.
